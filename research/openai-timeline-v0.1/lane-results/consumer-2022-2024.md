# Consumer and model timeline lane: 2022–2024

## Scope and method

This lane covers official OpenAI consumer, model, client, multimodal, search, memory, enterprise, safety, and adjacent product events from ChatGPT's public launch on 2022-11-30 through 2024-12-31. It contains 110 event records in [`consumer-2022-2024.json`](./consumer-2022-2024.json).

Only first-party OpenAI sources were used. Dated entries in living release notes were atomized into distinct product events, then supplemented with direct launch posts, model notes, client changelogs, enterprise notes, and the official 12 Days archive. A date represents the official announcement or stated rollout date; it does not necessarily mean that every account or country received the capability that day. Sources were retrieved on 2026-07-13.

## Principal evidence

The origin record is anchored to [Introducing ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt/), which describes the 2022-11-30 research preview. The 2023 shift from a single chat product toward a paid, multimodal, extensible platform is supported by [ChatGPT Plus](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-plus/), the [GPT-4 research release](https://openai.com/index/gpt-4-research/), [ChatGPT plugins](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-plugins/), [voice and image capabilities](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-can-now-see-hear-and-speak/), [DALL-E 3 availability](https://openai.com/index/dall-e-3-is-now-available-in-chatgpt-plus-and-enterprise/), and the [DevDay model and product announcement](https://openai.com/index/new-models-and-developer-products-announced-at-devday/). Confidence: high.

The 2024 expansion is anchored by direct posts for [Memory](https://openai.com/index/memory-and-new-controls-for-chatgpt/), [GPT-4o](https://openai.com/index/hello-gpt-4o/), [GPT-4o mini](https://openai.com/index/gpt-4o-mini-advancing-cost-efficient-intelligence/), [SearchGPT](https://openai.com/index/searchgpt-prototype/), [OpenAI o1](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-o1-preview/), [Canvas](https://openai.com/index/introducing-canvas/), [ChatGPT Search](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-search/), [ChatGPT Pro](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-pro/), and the public [Sora launch](https://openai.com/index/sora-is-here/). Confidence: high.

Granular rollout and client history comes from the official [ChatGPT Release Notes](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/6825453-chatgpt-release-notes), [Model Release Notes](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/9624314-model-release-notes), [Windows app release notes](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/10003026-windows-app-release-notes), [macOS app release notes](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/9703738-desktop-app-release-notes), [Enterprise and Edu release notes](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/10128477-chatgpt-enterprise-edu-release-notes), and the official [12 Days of OpenAI archive](https://openai.com/12-days/). Confidence: high for the text observed at retrieval time; historical stability is lower because several are living documents.

## Ambiguities and apparent contradictions

- Living changelogs can be edited retroactively, renamed, or pruned. The dataset records what the official pages exposed at retrieval time, not a cryptographically immutable historical snapshot.
- Preview, rollout, general availability, and later plan or geography expansion are separate lifecycle events. Repeated appearances of Memory, Browse/Search, DALL-E, Voice, and desktop capabilities are intentional rather than duplicate rows.
- Historical names are preserved. Code Interpreter later became Advanced Data Analysis, and ChatGPT Team was renamed ChatGPT Business in 2025; the 2023–2024 records retain the names used at the time.
- The February 2024 Sora entry is a research preview; 2024-12-09 is the standalone product launch. Likewise, the 2024-12-20 o3/o3-mini item is an announced preview and early-access program, not general availability.
- The GPT-4o mini launch post explicitly supports its replacement of GPT-3.5 in ChatGPT on 2024-07-18. API availability is adjacent evidence and should remain a separate event in the platform lane.
- OpenAI frequently stages releases across plans, platforms, and regions. An official release-note date should not be interpreted as universal rollout completion.

## Known gaps

- This is an official-source baseline, not proof that every experiment, silent model change, or country-by-country rollout was documented publicly.
- Patch-level Windows and macOS fixes were included selectively when they represented a distinct product capability; minor bug fixes were not atomized exhaustively.
- Service incidents, social-only announcements, undocumented A/B tests, and third-party reporting were excluded.
- The September 2023 DALL-E 3 research announcement was not added as a separate dated row because the browsed official evidence did not provide a sufficiently stable dated record. October beta and availability events are present.
- API-only SDK, quota, and deprecation changes belong in the platform/developer lane and should be reconciled there rather than duplicated here.
- The two `indexed` records—1-800-CHATGPT and the o3/o3-mini preview—should be upgraded if a dated immutable first-party launch page is recovered.

## Recommended follow-up checks

1. Snapshot or hash every living changelog before publishing the canonical dataset.
2. Diff the official site index and any first-party RSS feeds against the 110 event dates to identify omissions.
3. Recover archived first-party dated evidence for the September 2023 DALL-E 3 announcement and the two `indexed` December 2024 records.
4. Reconcile this lane against platform/developer and 2025–2026 lanes using date, product, lifecycle, and source—not title alone. Preserve consumer and API availability as separate events when the surfaces or audiences differ.
5. If the map supports minor nodes, run a second pass that atomizes every Windows and macOS patch-level entry; otherwise keep those details in expandable source panels.

## Handoff note

The JSON is chronological and designed for deterministic merging. `event_id` is unique within the lane, `importance` is an integer from 1–5, `tags` is always an array, and every source URL is restricted to an official OpenAI domain. The merger should retain lifecycle distinctions and avoid collapsing announcement, preview, rollout, and general-availability records into a single node.
