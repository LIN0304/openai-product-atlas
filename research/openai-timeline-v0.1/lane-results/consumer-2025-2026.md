3. The living ChatGPT release-notes page is not sufficient by itself. Major launches including Operator, deep research, native 4o image generation, Codex, ChatGPT agent, Sora 2, apps in ChatGPT, Atlas, GPT-5.2, GPT-5.2-Codex, GPT-5.5, and the GPT-5.6 family require official launch posts, model notes, or the unified product release feed.
4. Separate lifecycle records are retained for launch, expansion, deprecation notice, and effective retirement when the official source gives those as distinct events.

## Contradictions, gaps, and exclusions

- **GPT-5-Codex-Mini date gap:** the current official Model Release Notes contains an undated heading for this launch. Because this lane requires a concrete date and no dated official OpenAI page was found, that record is excluded rather than inferring a date from third-party mirrors.
- **Living-page revision risk:** Help Center changelogs are edited in place. `retrieved_at=2026-07-13` is therefore carried on every record, and older wording may differ from earlier snapshots.
- **Sora lifecycle:** official Sora launch pages were retroactively annotated to say the product ended April 26, 2026. The discontinuation record uses OpenAI's dedicated Help Center article as the direct source.
- **Patch-level completeness:** titled ChatGPT release-note entries are covered, plus several untitled major launch/lifecycle events. A few untitled February-March 2025 mobile bug-fix bundles were not atomized into individual product events because they lack stable official titles and are low-significance patch notes.
- **Lane boundary:** granular API changelog entries, API pricing, Codex CLI point releases, Business/Enterprise admin-only notes, and macOS/Windows patch-level changelogs belong to the platform/lifecycle lane and are not duplicated here unless they materially affected consumer or model surfaces.
